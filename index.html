Branding

The center of the diamond. Everything radiates from here. We're measuring brand clarity, consistency, architecture, internal adoption, competitive positioning, and promise delivery.

QUESTIONS 1–10 OF 44

Your brand positioning is clearly defined — what you stand for, who you serve, how you're different.

Your brand is consistent across all touchpoints — visual identity, messaging, tone, client experience.

You have clear guidelines on what your brand should NOT do — the negative space is protected.

Your team lives the brand internally — culture, values, and client interactions align with how you present externally.

Your ideal client can describe what you stand for in one sentence.

Your brand architecture is clean — sub-brands, service lines, and product categories are logically structured and don't confuse the market.

You can clearly articulate how you're differentiated from your top 3 competitors — and the market agrees.

Your brand promise is consistently delivered at every stage of the client journey — from first impression to offboarding.

You have a system for measuring brand perception — surveys, NPS, social listening, or feedback loops are in place.

Your brand has a clear evolution strategy — you know what the brand needs to become in the next 12–24 months and have a plan to get there.

Credibility

The left wall of the diamond. Can you prove you're as good as you say? We're measuring validation, thought leadership, proof infrastructure, peer recognition, and authority signals.

QUESTIONS 11–18 OF 44

You have recognized industry credentials, certifications, or awards that validate your expertise.

You are regularly featured as a thought leader — publications, podcasts, speaking engagements, or industry panels.

You have documented case studies or proven results that prospective clients can reference.

Industry peers refer clients to you based on your reputation and track record.

Prospective clients would describe you as an authority in your field before ever working with you.

You have a consistent publishing cadence — articles, whitepapers, frameworks, or thought pieces appear on a predictable schedule.

You maintain an active award and recognition pipeline — you're regularly submitting and winning industry recognition.

Your credibility extends beyond you personally — your company or brand has institutional authority independent of any single individual.

Visibility

The right wall. Are you seen — strategically, by the right people, in the right places? We're measuring media presence, platform strategy, search positioning, content systems, and executive visibility.

QUESTIONS 19–28 OF 44

Potential clients know you exist before they have a need — you're on their radar already.

You have a consistent, strategic content or media presence — not just occasional posts, but a system.

You have been featured in relevant media, publications, or platforms where your buyers pay attention.

You are present and visible in the spaces where your ideal clients gather — events, communities, industry circles.

When someone searches for solutions you provide, they find you — not just your competitors.

Your LinkedIn presence (personal or company) is actively driving inbound conversations, not just broadcasting.

You have established relationships with journalists, editors, or podcast hosts in your industry vertical.

Your content distribution system is multi-channel — you repurpose and distribute across at least 3 platforms strategically.

Your SEO and search presence goes beyond your brand name — you rank for problem-based and solution-based queries.

Executive visibility and company brand visibility are balanced — the market knows both the leadership and the organization.

Market Trust

The foundation of the diamond. Brand equity. Consumer confidence. We're measuring client loyalty, referral velocity, pricing power, reputation resilience, and perception alignment.

QUESTIONS 29–36 OF 44

Clients choose you over competitors with confidence — not because you're cheapest, but because they trust you.

Your client retention rate is strong — clients stay and the relationship deepens over time.

You receive unsolicited referrals regularly — people recommend you without being asked.

You can command premium pricing without significant pushback — clients understand and accept your value.

Clients would publicly endorse you — testimonials, reviews, case study participation — without hesitation.

You systematically measure client satisfaction — NPS, CSAT, or structured feedback loops are active and reviewed.

Your brand can withstand a crisis — you've built enough trust equity that a misstep wouldn't destroy your reputation.

The market's perception of your brand closely matches your intended positioning — there's minimal gap between how you see yourself and how others see you.

Demand

The peak of the diamond. Revenue. Inbound interest. Market pull. We're measuring pipeline predictability, acquisition efficiency, revenue growth, client quality, and opportunity magnetism.

QUESTIONS 37–44 OF 44

Your inbound inquiry volume is growing — people are coming to you, not just the other way around.

You are at or near capacity — you're turning away work or being selective about who you take on.

Your revenue is growing year over year without proportionally increasing operational effort.

You are attracting your ideal clients — not just anyone, but the ones your brand is designed to serve.

Opportunities come to you — partnerships, press, speaking invitations, deals — without you chasing all of them.

Your sales pipeline is predictable — you can forecast revenue with confidence based on current inbound velocity.

Your client acquisition cost is trending down over time as market authority compounds.

Your revenue streams are diversified — you're not dependent on a single client, channel, or service for the majority of income.
